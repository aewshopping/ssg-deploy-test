A slightly sparser list of new history books this month (Feb 2026, in hardback in the UK). The two that I am curious to find out a bit more about are:

- __Red Dawn Over China: How Communism Conquered a Quarter of Humanity__, by _Frank Dikötter_. The thesis being that the Communists in China were no different to their Nationalist Guomingdang opponents, so there was no 'moral' component to their victory. Having not yet read the book I am not yet convinced!

- __The Death of Trotsky: The True Story of the Plot to Kill Stalin's Greatest Enemy__, by _Josh Ireland_. Another compelling / horrific movie plot from the past. I expect an exciting, made for cinema style book.
